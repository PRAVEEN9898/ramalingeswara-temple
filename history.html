Temple History

400+ Years of Divine Legacy

The Sacred Origins

Nestled in the serene village of Selapadu, Sri Ramalingeswara Swamy Temple was established around 1600 AD by local devotees who experienced divine visions of Lord Shiva. The temple's Lingam is believed to be self-manifested (Swayambhu), making it a powerful spiritual center.

Over centuries, the temple has served as the cultural and religious hub of Chebrolu Mandal, hosting major festivals and attracting devotees from across Andhra Pradesh.

1600 AD - Construction

Temple founded with Swayambhu Lingam installation

1800 AD - Renovation

Major structural renovations during Vijayanagara era
